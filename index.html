About

After graduating with my undergraduate degree, hopping on a plane two days later to start a hectic month-long trip around Europe, and finally coming back home, I sat there and started to apply for jobs. I browsed around LinkedIn and Indeed to see what was available, and as I kept scrolling down and down, not many jobs really interested me. It made me wonder, if I actually wanted to find a job in the field of my degree.

During my time at the university, I did fine academically, but I didn’t feel emotionally invested in it. I didn’t wake up in the morning excited to go to class or run out of class after lecture anxiously rushing to sit down and do my homework. Instead, I went home, thought about how I had even more class the next day, and wondered how many days were left until the weekend. “That’s just how I am, unable to feel too excited about things. It’s normal for me. It’s how I am.” I would tell myself and then I would go through the motions.

I'm always trying to figure out life.

During and after my trip around Europe, I was confused. Here, it was supposed to be a trip people dream about going to. Then there’s me, I went through the motions. I searched for the top things to do in an area. I saw all the massive architecture that popped up seemingly out of nowhere as I exited a random alley, I ate food specialized in the area, and I socialized. But at the end of each day, I sat there and thought about it. I asked myself, “Did you find that enjoyable? Did you have fun?” Being honest with myself, I didn’t really think so. “Must just be how I am.”

A habit I have is searching Reddit or asking others about their thoughts on how things are supposed to be. If someone else has fun, I want to know how they're doing it and what makes their experience different. I'm always trying to figure out life, so I asked. I asked my friends, and I looked at various posts. If you traveled to Europe, what would you do for fun? It’s a question I’ll probably be asking everyone from now on. Of course, there are going to be varying answers.

Back to my job search, I've scrolled past so many job postings, and I’m struggling. Not because I don’t know the motions to find a job. Not because my resume is weak. Not because my GPA is low. It’s much more difficult than that. It’s because I don’t find the excitement in the work. I have a fear that it will be a job where I wake up and dread getting out of bed to get to. Where I count the days until the super short 2-day weekend to start it all over again. So here I am. I’m not sure why I’m learning to code a website or posting a long story about myself. I should find a job. But, here I am. I want to find that thing everyone else probably does: good old-fashioned fulfillment. The drive to do what makes me want to get out of bed in the morning.
